# Customers and positioning (Anton)

## Who feels this pain most

Everyone who's ever started a new job felt it. But it bites hardest where:

- roles are distinct enough that one checklist can't serve them (developers vs.
  everyone else, especially),
- the company hires often enough that no single person can sit next to every
  new hire and walk them through setup,
- the "how things work here" knowledge lives in people's heads and scattered
  Slack threads, not in one current place.

That points to **growing tech companies and scale-ups** — big enough that
informal "Petya will show you around" onboarding has broken, not big enough to
have a dedicated L&D machine. Roughly 30–300 people, hiring steadily.

## What we're actually selling

Not a knowledge base. Not a chatbot. Not an HR checklist tool.

We're selling **the person who sits next to you on day one** — at scale. The
thing every new hire wishes they had (someone who sees their screen and says
"click here, now this") and that no company can staff for every hire. That's the
product: a companion that does the hands-on part a document can never do.

## Buyers, in priority order

1. **Founders / team leads at small fast-growing companies.** They feel this
   most directly and personally — every new hire currently costs *them* hours of
   hand-holding, or worse, gets dropped and ramps slowly. They're also the ones
   who'll appreciate the "ideal flow": send an email, the new person installs a
   buddy, and onboarding just happens. This is our first wedge.

2. **Engineering managers.** Every new developer eats hours of their week:
   environment help, "how does deploy work," "who owns this," repeated for every
   hire. The hands-on dev onboarding plus stage-two context takes that off their
   plate, and they show up to the first 1:1 talking about work.

3. **People / HR leadership.** They own the onboarding experience and feel its
   failure as a metric (slow ramp, bad first-week NPS). Jarvis gives them
   something that actually does the onboarding instead of another doc nobody
   reads — and it's the same for every hire, every time, without depending on a
   senior person having a free afternoon.

## How we're different

**It does the part a doc can't.** Everyone else's "onboarding" is content — a
better wiki, a checklist app, a chatbot over the docs. Ours is a guide on your
screen who watches where you are and drives the cursor. That's a different
category: not "where's the information," but "walk me through it."

**It's a presence, not a destination.** A character that lives on your screen
gets used in the stuck moment. A portal you have to remember to open does not.

**It's honest.** When it doesn't know, it routes you to the human who probably
does, with the reason. It doesn't pretend to be the source of all truth — it's a
great first ask and a good router.

**It builds its own living context.** Drop him into meetings and he keeps the
transcripts, so his knowledge is what the team actually said this week — not a
stale wiki. The longer he's around, the more real spoken context he holds. That
compounding context is the moat.

**You talk to him.** Fast voice (Wispr-Flow-style) makes asking him quicker than
the workaround. If it's slower than guessing, nobody uses it — so speed is the
product, and voice is how we win it.

**It's scoped on purpose.** We're not promising automated access magic out of
the gate. We're promising the thing people actually feel the absence of on day
one: a buddy who does it with you.

## The comparison set

Buyers may compare Jarvis to:

- **Onboarding doc / Notion checklist** — tells, never shows; goes stale; can't
  see your screen or answer the follow-up. This is the status quo we replace.
- **HR onboarding tools** (Workday, BambooHR flows) — paperwork and checklists,
  not a hands-on guide through the actual screens.
- **Generic AI chatbots** — no company context, no screen awareness, can't walk
  you through setup. Good for trivia, useless for "set up your environment here."
- **A human buddy** — the thing that actually works, which is exactly what
  doesn't scale. We're the scalable version of the one approach everyone agrees
  is best.

The gap: nobody does the hands-on, screen-aware, do-it-with-you part. That's the
whole bet.
