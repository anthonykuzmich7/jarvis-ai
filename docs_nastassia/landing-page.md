# Landing page — Nastassia's vision

## Goal

Before building the product, validate that the **operational, performance-framed
pitch** lands with the right buyer (COO / Head of Operations at growing 100+
person companies). The page should make that buyer feel: "this shrinks
time-to-performance and gives me more top performers."

---

## Core message

**Onboard your best people so their time-to-performance shrinks — and grow more
top performers inside your team.**

The way to do it: give every new hire a chief of staff that loads the context of
your company and their team during onboarding, then keeps building their personal
context and acting on their behalf.

---

## Hero — what we're testing

Two hero angles, both worth validating (run them together as one A/B):

- **A:** "How do you onboard your best employees so their time-to-performance
  shrinks?"
- **B:** "How do you grow more top performers inside your team?"

Primary action: see CTA below.

---

## CTA — test both

Put **both** a waitlist (email capture) and a **book-a-call** option on the site
and A/B test them:

- **Waitlist** — lowest friction, clean read on interest.
- **Book a call** — higher friction, but qualifies a B2B buyer like a COO much
  better.

We want to learn which the right people actually choose.

---

## Page structure

### 1. Hero — the outcome, not the feature
Lead with the performance framing (A and B above), aimed squarely at ops
leadership. Subhead: every new hire gets a chief of staff with context from day
one.

### 2. The problem — context doesn't exist yet
The context a new hire needs was never assembled — it's in heads, calls, and
scattered docs. Generic onboarding fails a developer and a marketer equally.
Make the reader feel the wasted first weeks and the senior time burned.

### 3. Meet Jarvis — the chief of staff
He's an agent, not a knowledge base. Minimalist, voice-first, lives where you
work (overlay + Slack), and he *acts*.

### 4. Persona-based demo — the "wow"
Let the visitor **pick a persona** and see a customized scenario where the agent
onboards by team context **and performs a real action**:

- **Engineer:** instantly get access to a new tool, or get a review of the
  current stack / architecture.
- **Creative marketer:** receive the brand knowledge — every format we've run,
  the concepts that actually worked, and who owns which format.
- **Business / C-level:** the agent explains the company vision, goals, and
  C-level plans, and schedules calls with the right stakeholders.

Pick three use cases (one per persona) and make it unmistakable that the agent
adapts to the team's context and acts like a real agent.

### 5. Two-stage arc — onboarding in, teammate forever
Stage 1: super-proactive onboarding that gets you set up. Stage 2: a sticky
teammate whose context (including your personal layer) keeps driving your daily
work.

### 6. Who it's for
Name the buyer: **COO and Head of Operations** at growing 100+ person companies —
people who own hiring throughput, productivity, and OKRs/KPIs.

### 7. CTA (repeat)
Both waitlist and book-a-call, framed as joining early teams.

### 8. FAQ / objections
Security and access control, data privacy, **context isolation between teams**,
what it connects to, and meeting-recording consent.

## Build notes

- One page to start; ship fast and iterate.
- Both CTAs must actually store leads, tagged so we can compare them.
- Persona demo can start as an animated mockup (e.g. designed in Figma, then
  animated) before any real product exists.
- Copy should sound human and specific, in the language of operations leaders —
  not generic AI-startup marketing.

---

## What to validate with this page

1. **Do the hero angles resonate** with the target audience (COO / Head of Ops)?
   This is the first thing to test — run A and B together.
2. **Which CTA converts** — waitlist vs. book-a-call?
3. **Does the persona-based, "it acts" demo** feel exciting and credible?
4. **Does the sticky / context angle** read as clearly different from one-time
   onboarding tools?
