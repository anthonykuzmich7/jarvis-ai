# Landing page — Palina's angle

## What I'd want the page to communicate

The current landing page leads with access and IT. That's real pain, but it's
not the whole story and it might not be the most relatable hook for everyone.

My angle: the first two weeks at a new company feel like being dropped in a
foreign city without a map. You know the destination, you don't know the route,
and everyone around you is too busy to give you a full tour.

Jarvis is the guide who meets you at the door.

## The message I'd test

**Headline direction:** "Your new hire's first two weeks — personalized."

Or more direct: "Generic onboarding doesn't work. Jarvis fixes that."

The sub-message: every role has different needs on day one. Jarvis knows yours.

## Page structure I'd try

1. **Hook** — the feeling of week one: lost, asking around, reading docs that
   don't apply to you. Make it viscerally familiar.

2. **The reframe** — onboarding isn't slow because companies don't care. It's
   slow because the same information can't serve everyone equally. Personalization
   is the missing piece.

3. **How Jarvis works** — show the overlay mechanic. Show Jarvis appearing in
   Slack. Show the mascot. Make it feel like something you'd actually want to use,
   not another corporate dashboard.

4. **The role personalization point** — one example: "Joining as a designer?
   Here's what Jarvis gives you on day one." vs. "Joining as a developer? Here's
   your day one." Side by side or in a toggle. Make the difference concrete.

5. **The long-term value** — brief: Jarvis doesn't leave when onboarding ends.
   Company knowledge, always current, always searchable.

6. **CTA** — waitlist, framed as: "We're working with early teams to get this
   right. Join the list."

## What I'd want to learn from the page

- Does the personalization angle resonate, or do people just nod at it without
  feeling the pain?
- Does the overlay / mascot mechanic feel interesting or gimmicky at this stage?
- Which role does the buyer imagine filling — HR, manager, or the new hire
  themselves?
