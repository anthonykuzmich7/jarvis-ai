# Jarvis AI — Vision (Palina)

> Working title: **Jarvis AI**. Name is not final.

## One line

Jarvis is a personalized corporate buddy — a desktop overlay you summon with a
keyboard shortcut or your voice — that knows your role, guides you through
onboarding step by step, and stays useful long after your first week.

## The metaphor: a buddy who actually knows you

Most companies have a buddy program on paper. In practice, that buddy is busy,
forgets to follow up, and gives you the same generic checklist everyone gets.

Jarvis is the buddy who shows up every time, knows you're a designer not a
developer, and adjusts everything accordingly. He sits on top of your existing
tools — Slack, Notion, your email — and nudges you exactly when you need it,
exactly in the context you're already in.

He's not a page you visit. He's a presence that follows your work.

## What it looks like

You join a company. On day one, Jarvis appears — triggered by a keyboard
shortcut or your voice, like Wispr Flow — and says: "Welcome. You're joining as
a Product Designer. Here's what matters for you specifically."

- He opens Slack and walks you through it: "This is your manager. Want me to
  help you draft a hello message?" "These three channels are the ones your team
  actually uses."
- He surfaces the company docs, roadmaps, and vision that are relevant to your
  role — not a 40-page PDF for everyone, but the five things you actually need
  to know today.
- He has a face — a mascot — so the whole experience feels like talking to
  someone, not clicking through a checklist. There's a small element of
  gamification: progress, milestones, a sense that you're actually getting
  somewhere.

## What makes it different

Existing onboarding is generic. HR sends the same doc to the new designer as to
the new backend engineer. The information is right but the relevance is wrong.

Jarvis knows your role before you ask. He pre-selects the documentation,
introduces you to the people you'll actually work with, and skips what doesn't
apply to you.

## After onboarding

Jarvis doesn't leave when the first two weeks are over. He becomes the company's
living knowledge base — a place where roadmaps, processes, and decisions get
added as they happen. You can ask him anything about the company at any point,
and the answer is current.

This is the long-term value: not a one-time onboarding tool, but a shareable
knowledge memory that makes tribal knowledge accessible to everyone.

## Why this matters

1. **New hires waste days gathering context** that should have been handed to
   them on day one — personalized, relevant, and actionable.
2. **Onboarding is not one-size-fits-all.** A developer, a designer, and a
   business analyst have completely different first-week needs. Generic docs
   fail all three.
3. **Company knowledge decays.** Roadmaps go stale, people leave, decisions get
   lost. Jarvis keeps it alive and searchable.
