# Customers and positioning (Palina)

## Who feels this pain most

The onboarding problem exists in every company that hires people. But the pain
is sharpest in companies where:

- roles are specialized and distinct (developers, designers, analysts, sales all
  have different first-week needs),
- the team is growing fast enough that senior people can't personally onboard
  everyone,
- knowledge is scattered across multiple tools (Confluence, Notion, Slack,
  Google Drive, Jira).

That points to **tech companies and scale-ups** as the primary market — teams
that are too big for informal onboarding but not yet big enough to have a
dedicated onboarding team.

## Buyers, in priority order

1. **People / HR leadership** — they own the onboarding process and feel the
   failure directly. When new hires are slow to ramp, it's HR's problem.
   Jarvis gives them something they can point to: onboarding that's
   personalized, measurable, and doesn't depend on a senior engineer having
   time.

2. **Engineering managers** — they absorb most of the informal onboarding
   burden. Every new developer needs 1:1s, environment help, and context that
   only the manager has. Jarvis takes that off their plate.

3. **Design and product leadership** — designers and PMs have their own
   onboarding gaps (design systems, research context, product strategy) that
   generic HR docs don't address. A head of design who's seen three new
   designers spend two weeks confused will immediately understand Jarvis.

4. **IT / DevOps** — access provisioning is one part of the picture. The deeper
   need is that new hires don't know what to ask for or how to ask for it.
   Jarvis handles the guidance layer so IT only deals with the actual
   provisioning.

## What we're selling

Not a knowledge base. Not a chatbot. Not another HR tool.

We're selling **the first week back** — the productivity new hires lose while
they're figuring out where everything is. And we're selling it to the people who
are currently paying for that loss without a clear way to fix it.

The pitch to HR: "Your new hires are spending their first week in 1:1s and
reading docs that don't apply to them. Jarvis gives everyone a personalized
guide on day one."

The pitch to engineering managers: "Every time someone new joins your team, you
spend four to six hours that week answering the same questions. Jarvis handles
those."

## How we're different

- **Not a generic help bot.** Jarvis knows your role before you ask. He
  pre-selects what's relevant and surfaces it at the right moment.
- **Not a knowledge base you have to visit.** He appears where you already are —
  in Slack, in your tools — and guides you in context.
- **Not a one-time onboarding checklist.** He stays useful as the company's
  living knowledge base, updated continuously.
- **Not impersonal.** The mascot, the gamification, the conversational overlay
  — all of it is designed to make the experience feel like talking to a person,
  not filling out a form.
