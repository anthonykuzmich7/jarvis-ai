# The problem

## Joining a company is slow and painful

When you join a new company, it can take around **two weeks** to get all the
accesses you need. During that time you're half-blocked: you can't run the
project, can't deploy, can't see the tools you're supposed to work in.

The reasons it's painful:

- **You don't know who to reach out to.** Should this access come from DevOps?
  From a team lead? From the platform team? Nobody tells you, so you guess.
- **The process is undocumented or scattered.** Bits live in Confluence, bits in
  Slack history, bits only in people's heads.
- **You waste hours waiting.** You ask in a channel, wait, get pointed somewhere
  else, wait again. The cost is hours per access, multiplied across every system.

## Beyond access: you don't know how anything works

Access is only half of it. As a new hire you also don't know:

- how **merge requests** get created here,
- how to **deploy a new service** (and whether you need DevOps to set something
  up first),
- **who owns what** and who to ask when you're stuck,
- how the **product actually works** — what it is, who it's for,
- how the company **makes money**.

Today this knowledge is tribal. It lives in senior people's heads and in stale
docs. Every new hire rebuilds it slowly, asking around, interrupting people.

## Who pays for this today

- **The new hire** loses their first one to two weeks of productivity.
- **IT / DevOps specialists** spend real time manually granting access and
  re-explaining the same processes.
- **The team** gets interrupted constantly to onboard the new person.

The pain is concrete and measurable: time-to-first-commit, time-to-first-deploy,
hours of IT effort per hire. Those are the numbers Jarvis should move.
