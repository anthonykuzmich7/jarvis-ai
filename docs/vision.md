# Jarvis AI — Vision

> Working title: **Jarvis AI**. Name is not final.

## One line

Jarvis is an AI teammate that IT companies can "hire" — a digital coworker that
lives inside your company's tools and helps people get onboarded, get their
accesses, and get context, without waiting on an IT specialist.

## The metaphor: a body you plug in

Think of Jarvis as a *body* — an agent a company drops into its environment. The
same Jarvis can show up in different forms depending on how you want to use him:

- as a **Slack user** you can DM,
- as a **real user account** inside your systems,
- connected to your cloud and tools through **MCP**, so something like Claude
  Code can talk to him directly.

You connect the body to your company's knowledge and systems, and he becomes a
real member of the team. He can be managed and configured differently for each
company.

## What he is, in plain terms

When you join a company, there's usually one person — a buddy, a mentor, the
helpful senior who answers your dumb questions for the first month. Jarvis is
that person, except:

- he's available instantly, to everyone,
- he never forgets the company's processes,
- he can actually *grant* the access instead of just telling you who to ask.

He's not a chatbot bolted onto a help center. He's meant to feel like a
colleague who already knows how the company works and already has the context of
what *you* are working on.

## Why this matters

Two real costs we're attacking:

1. **New hires lose their first weeks.** Getting accesses can take ~2 weeks.
   People sit blocked, not knowing who to ask.
2. **IT specialists lose time.** Manually provisioning access and answering the
   same onboarding questions over and over is expensive and repetitive.

If Jarvis removes most of that, both sides win: the new person is productive on
day one or two, and IT gets their time back.
